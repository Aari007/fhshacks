About FHS.hacks()

FHS.hacks() is Franklin High School’s annual hackathon. A hackathon is an opportunity where students get together to develop creative solutions to real world problems. During the hackathon, students will get access to mentors, workshops, other student coders, as well as “hacking” time to turn their ideas into reality. FHS.hacks() is open to all high school students from anywhere in the US with competitive and innovative spirits. Whether you’re a new coder or a seasoned hacker, come join the FHS WARRIORS for a weekend of learning, connection, and fun!

Workshop - Reinforcement Learning by Professor Schwartz

Prof. Mathew Schwartz got his Bachelor of Fine Arts in 2011 and MSc in Architecture in 2012 at University of Michigan, after which he was a research scientist at Seoul National University's Interdisciplinary Research Institute for 4 years. During this time he worked with Prof. Jaeheung Park designing humanoid robots and building motion analysis tools. In 2017 he joined New Jersey Institute of Technologies College of Architecture and Design. His research focuses on computational design, combining the study of human factors and construction techniques to make pre-occupancy evaluation tools and novel manufacturing approaches to improve the built environment for its users.

His talk will focus on Reinforcement Learning - which is the latest way people are using Machine Learning.

Workshop - Cybersecurity by Caitlin Boyce

Caitlin Boyce is a Detection Engineer at the industrial cybersecurity company Dragos, Inc. where she designs analytics for the Dragos Platform to detect a range of malicious activities and characterize ICS assets, to enable ICS owners and operators to better protect their infrastructure against threats. Before joining Dragos, Caitlin worked with SANS Institute’s CyberTalent Team supporting scholarship-based immersion academies with program management and as a subject matter expert. She has also been an instructor at the University of New Orleans where she taught Systems Programming Concepts. A graduate of the 2018 SANS Women’s Immersion Academy, she is also a member of the GIAC Advisory Board and has interned with Cisco as a Security Solutions Engineer.

Speaker - Quantum Computing

Qubit by Qubit is a quantum education nonprofit and a global leader in quantum computing education. We work with leading partners, like IBM Quantum, Google, and Caltech to expand access to opportunities to learn about quantum – and we are proudly the newest member of the White House’s National Q-12 Partnership. From our year-long quantum computing course to one-time workshops, Qubit by Qubit has taught nearly 15,000 students quantum computing from 125 countries. Quantum computing will impact industries from healthcare to finance to cybersecurity, and we want today’s high school students to develop a foundational understanding of quantum computing and be prepared to contribute to the next computing revolution.
